About me

Hi, I am Hashir Ahmad. A graduate student at the Technical University of Munich. I am currently working as a Graduate Assistant at Max Planck Institute of Neurobiology. My work is focused on Connectomics where our research group is performing automated synaptic connectivity inference based on dense EM segmentation data. Previously, I was employed as a Working Student at Huawei Munich Research Center where I worked on modeling thermogram data and operational parameters of a 2x2 RF Chipset.

Additionally, I mentor undergraduate students as part of the SSGSA (Sir Syed Global Scholar Award) organization. In practical terms, my goal is to guide the selected scholars in their admission processes into Universities.
